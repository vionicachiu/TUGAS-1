  • Review: Jason Mraz releases feel-good sixth album 'Know.'

    • read from www.redandblack.com

    Singer-songwriter Jason Mraz released his sixth studio album, "Know.," on Aug. 10. The album is packed with upbeat tracks and tongue-twisting lyrics and explores the theme of love. The pop album comes four years after his last studio album, "YES!" and in an interview with Billboard, Mraz revealed that much of the album was inspired by love letters he wrote to his wife and also himself "to constantly remind myself that love is still the answer."

  • Review: Ed Sheeran's 'Divide' brings pop and folk sounds

    • read from www.redandblack.com

    After a yearlong hiatus, Ed Sheeran has released his third album "÷" – read as "divide." Despite creating distinct eras through the math symbol stylization pattern, "Divide" does not significantly vary from the "Multiply" ("x") or "Plus" ("+") era. "Divide" flickers back and forth between Sheeran's heart-felt ballads and distinctive acoustic, hip hop songs. A notable difference is the incorporation of folk-inspired sounds.

  • 'The Greatest Showman' Soundtrack Review

    • read from www.pluggedin.com

    Weeks after the inspiring musical movie's theatrical release, so many fans began adding their own voices to the mix that 20th Century Fox began to schedule special sing-along screenings. That cultural phenomena (along with exposure at this year's Golden Globes awards) has helped to propel the soundtrack to the pinnacle of Billboard's album chart. In fact, it's the first movie soundtrack to hold the top spot for two weeks in a row since the one for Suicide Squad accomplished that feat in the summer of 2016, according to The New York Times. Though the movie's storyline is fraught with some historical innacuracies, the songs themselves celebrate messages of empowerment and personal dignity that have the ability to span centuries.
